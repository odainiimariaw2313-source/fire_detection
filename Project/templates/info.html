Information

Especially in case of high fire risk:

Designated Areas Only

Fires can only be lit in specially designated areas

Constant Supervision

The fire must be constantly supervised

Safe Distance

Fires are not made near wood, leaves, or dry grass

Emergency Equipment

Have blankets and water on hand to extinguish out-of-control fires

Proper Extinguishing

Sprinkle ashes with water and cover with earth before leaving

Cigarette Safety

The remains of lit cigarettes are not thrown away randomly

Local Regulations

Check local regulations before burning yard waste, avoid windy days

Delimit Fire Area

Delimit the edges of the fire with stones or by digging a small hole

Protect nature by following wildfire prevention rules—every small action helps preserve forests and wildlife for the future.

Respecting fire safety norms means protecting lives, ecosystems, and the beauty of our environment.

Stay responsible and follow the rules. Together we can prevent wildfires and keep nature safe.
